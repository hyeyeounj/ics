Can colours be used to segment words when reading?

Manuel Perea, Universitat de Valencia, Valencia, Spain

Written texts are typically presented in black and white. However, colors help detect/recognize objects, and hence, colors can potentially be used as word dividers during reading. Here we tested whether color information produces a benefit on the reading process. Experiment 1 showed that unspaced, alternating color sentences only produced a small reading cost relative to the spaced sentences in adult readers; unspaced monocolor sentences showed a large reading cost. Thus, colors can be used to delimit word boundaries. Using spaced text, Experiment 2 did not show any advantage of multi-colored over mono-colored text when reading aloud. Experiment 3 was parallel to Experiment 2 except that it was conducted in Chinese, an unspaced writing system. We found no effect of color in adult readers. We examine the implications of these data in the context of literacy training.
